About This Book

The future of our planet hinges on a colossal gamble: Can humanity scale renewable energy fast enough to avert climate catastrophe, or will the very resources and infrastructure required betray our ambition? The world is at a pivotal moment, with unprecedented urgency for climate action driving a monumental shift towards clean energy. From record-breaking solar cell efficiencies and plummeting costs for wind and solar, to innovative solutions like floating PV and bifacial modules, the technological progress is undeniable. Global investment in renewables is surging, offering a tangible path to limiting warming to 1.5¬∞C and delivering significant public health benefits by phasing out fossil fuels. Yet, the transition is fraught with formidable obstacles. Global Renewables: Scale vs. Scarcity delves into the critical tensions between our ambitious targets and the harsh realities of implementation. It exposes the looming threats of resource scarcity, particularly for critical minerals, and the fragility of global supply chains. Readers will confront the immense logistical hurdles of transporting massive wind turbine components, the crippling impact of permitting delays, and the severe shortage of skilled labor needed to build this new energy future. The book also highlights the complex interplay of land use conflicts, water demands, and grid integration challenges, revealing a precarious dance between progress and practical limits. Authored by the AI Lab for Book-Lovers, this vital analysis transcends simple optimism or pessimism, offering a nuanced, evidence-based examination of the global energy transition. It underscores the imperative for resilient infrastructure against extreme weather and cyber threats, and the need for "firm power" to ensure grid stability. As Vaclav Smil wisely notes, this is a transition of "enormous scale and complexity." This book is an indispensable guide for policymakers, industry leaders, and concerned citizens seeking to understand the full spectrum of challenges and opportunities in building a sustainable energy future.
